Project 2.3.2: West Point Bridge Designer, POE

March 8, 2019 - March 21, 2019

Our goal of this project was to create a final bridge design under three hundred thousand dollars, using the West Point Bridge Designer software. We wanted to make a bridge that was able to withstand the weight of a truck, with the the least amount of tension and compression at each beam. My partner and I had to make two bridge ideas separately and then create a final design by modifying the best bridge. During the project, my main role was to make modifications to our brainstorm ideas to design a final bridge that was efficient and cost effective. To do this, I created a decision matrix, based on sturdiness, cost, aesthetics, and environmental impact, which helped us pick a base, for our bridge. I made the chosen design have thicker and stronger beams, at the areas with the most compression and tension. I also reduced the size of the beams that were least affected by tension and compression in order to keep the cost low. All these modifications allowed us to create a bridge that was not only cheap and sturdy, but also aesthetically pleasing. During this project, I learned how to work effectively with my partner by combining both aspects of our individual bridges to make a better and stronger bridge. Throughout the duration of the project, I learned how to use the West Point Bridge Designer software to create a variety of bridges, with arch abutments, piers, and deck elevations. Coming up with the final design was the most challenging part of the project, because there were times when my bridge would be sturdy, but too expensive. Finding a perfect balance between bridge stability and the cost limit was time consuming, but luckily I planned my time effectively. The project taught me how useful it is to use simulations to envision how my ideas will work in the real world.
